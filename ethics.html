Ethical Analysis of UFVCoin

Applying the nine ethical theories to show why UFVCoin is a responsible, community-focused initiative.

1. Subjective Relativism

Ethical: Individuals may view UFVCoin as ethical if it helps them save money, learn investing, or participate in a trusted student community.

Non-ethical: Some may feel UFVCoin is risky or unnecessary, seeing it as potentially encouraging poor financial choices.

2. Cultural Relativism

Ethical: In UFV culture, promoting financial literacy, student collaboration, and responsible investing aligns with community values.

Non-ethical: If the broader community views crypto as speculative or unsafe.

3. Divine Command

Ethical: Promotes fairness, supports students, encourages sharing wealth.

Non-ethical: Mismanagement could harm students financially.

4. Ethical Egoism

Ethical: Benefits university and stakeholders, aligns with self-interest.

Non-ethical: If it only benefited developers and caused risk.

5. Kantianism

Ethical: Treats students as ends, provides meaningful opportunities.

Non-ethical: Exploiting students for profit violates principles.

6. Act Utilitarianism

Ethical: Increases overall happiness through rewards, learning, community engagement.

Non-ethical: Causes stress or financial loss for some students.

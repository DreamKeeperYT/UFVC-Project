Ethical Analysis of UFVCoin

Applying the nine ethical theories to show why UFVCoin is a responsible, community-focused initiative.

1. Subjective Relativism

Ethical: Individuals may view UFVCoin as ethical if it helps them save money, learn investing, or participate in a trusted student community.

Non-ethical: Some may feel UFVCoin is risky or unnecessary, seeing it as potentially encouraging poor financial choices, which makes it unethical for them personally.

2. Cultural Relativism

Ethical: In UFV culture, promoting financial literacy, student collaboration, and responsible investing aligns with community values, making UFVCoin ethical.

Non-ethical: If the broader community views crypto as speculative or unsafe, UFVCoin may be considered unethical from a societal standpoint.

3. Divine Command

Ethical: If UFVCoin promotes fairness, supports students, and encourages sharing wealth, it aligns with values of helping others and can be seen as ethically positive.

Non-ethical: If mismanaged, UFVCoin could harm students financially, which may conflict with moral teachings about protecting and supporting the vulnerable.

4. Ethical Egoism

Ethical: UFVCoin benefits the university and stakeholders, allowing users to gain rewards, learn investing, and grow their wealth, aligning with self-interest.

Non-ethical: If the app only benefited developers or the university, and caused financial risk for students, it would be unethical from an egoist perspective.

5. Kantianism

Ethical: UFVCoin treats students as ends by providing meaningful opportunities to invest, learn, and participate in a transparent system.

Non-ethical: If students were exploited or misled for profit, UFVCoin would violate Kantian principles by using people merely as a means to an end.

6. Act Utilitarianism

Ethical: UFVCoin increases overall happiness by giving students rewards, financial learning, and community engagement.

Non-ethical: If the coin caused stress or financial loss for some students, the overall happiness would decrease, making it unethical under this theory.

7. Rule Utilitarianism

Ethical: If all universities offered similar student crypto programs, it would likely improve student financial literacy and happiness globally.

Non-ethical: If a universal rule of university crypto programs led to exploitation or gambling risks, it would be unethical under rule utilitarianism.

8a. Social Contract Theory

Ethical: UFVCoin follows rules designed for student benefit, respecting rights and contributing to the common good.

Non-ethical: Violating trust or unfairly restricting access would break the social contract, making the program unethical.

8b. Rawls’ Theory of Justice

Ethical: UFVCoin benefits all students and especially supports the least advantaged by offering rewards, scholarships, and opportunities to learn investing.

Non-ethical: If only wealthy students gained benefits while vulnerable students were excluded, it would violate Rawls’ principles.

9. Virtue Ethics

Ethical: UFVCoin encourages virtues like responsibility, generosity, and community engagement through volunteering and cooperative rewards.

Non-ethical: If students only focused on personal gain and ignored ethical investing or sharing, UFVCoin would not foster virtuous behavior.
